Beyond tariffs: A better approach to green industrial policy

January 30, 2026 · with T. Gerarden, B. Bollinger, and K. Gillingham

US solar panel tariffs since 2012 induced Chinese manufacturers to relocate production rather than exit markets, raising domestic prices, reducing employment, and hindering climate goals — production subsidies would do far better.

Search and information frictions on global e-commerce platforms: Evidence from AliExpress

December 15, 2020 · with J. Bai, M. Chen, and J. Liu

Search and information barriers on platforms like AliExpress prevent consumers from finding higher-quality sellers, allowing luck rather than quality to determine which firms succeed in international markets.

Related paper: Market Congestion and Information Frictions on Global E-Commerce Platforms
VoxEU

How relabelling increases the fiscal cost of R&D incentives

August 28, 2018 · with Z. Chen, Z. Liu, and J.C. Suárez Serrato

About 30% of reported R&D increases from China's tax incentive program resulted from firms relabeling non-R&D expenses, substantially raising the true fiscal cost of encouraging genuine innovation.

Related paper: Notching R&D Investment with Corporate Income Tax Cuts in China

Paying to Pollute: How Carbon Offsets Actually Raised Emissions in China

October 29, 2025 · with Q. Chen and N. Ryan

Firms receiving CDM carbon offset projects in China increased emissions by 49% rather than achieving projected 20% reductions, resulting in roughly $65 billion in global welfare losses.

Related paper: Firm Selection and Growth in Carbon Offset Markets

Evidence from China's 2008 corporate tax reform reveals strategic "notching" behavior, where firms bunch just above R&D thresholds to exploit tax incentives, with some relabeling of administrative expenses as R&D.

Who Pays for Tariffs Along the Supply Chain? Evidence from European Wine Tariffs

November 6, 2025 · with A. Flaaen, A. Hortaçsu, F. Tintelnot, and N. Urdaneta

Using 2019 US tariffs on European wines, this piece shows that consumers bore more than 100% of the tariff burden in dollar terms, while firms engaged in strategic product relabeling to minimize duties.
