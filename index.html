Research overview

Structured IO / quantitative trade + credible policy variation

My research lies at the intersection of International Trade, Industrial Organization, and Development. I use large-scale microdata to model and estimate (often dynamic) firm decisions and study how these choices shape resource allocation, industry performance, and growth—especially in developing and emerging economies.

I am interested in methodologies combining structural IO / quantitative trade with quasi-random policy variation and field experiments. More recently, I study the role of firms in environmental and energy regulation, green industrial policy, and offset markets.
